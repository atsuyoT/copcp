Identifying Critical SystemsüëÄ

What factors would need to be considerd in determining whether this new system
will be critical to the business and what the impact might be if it fails?


Good System Need

  1. Reduce operating costs (labour cost, etc)

    • Improved operational efficiency as it is connected to a network and can be linked to any system
    • Access from anywhere, making it possible to open the system to the public, not only within the company but also outside
    • The more time you can spare for operational tasks, so the more you can focus on customer analysis and business strategy

  2. Enable accurate and quick processing
    • Easy and automatically get report daily profit and lost using a system, and system data back up
    • Reduce careless mistakes/human errors by manual work
    • Almost no human error due to manual work as each data is automatically aggregated and accumulated

  3. Centralized management of management resources
    • No need to set aside space to store numerous data
    • Easy to maintain and manage a variety of information such as products, customers, sales, etc.

  4. Easy to communicate of information inside(employees)/contact outside (stakeholders & customers)

Bad Side System

  1. High cost of introduction/ large capital investment
    • Reduce costs enough to exceed initial system development costs and running costs by improving business efficiency.

  2. The system may not work well
    • Reduce costs enough to exceed initial system development costs and running costs by improving business efficiency.
    • Expertise may be required to actually use or manage the system: Consider employees' abilities, experiences and qualifications who will be in charge of the work. Need to take care to reduce their anxiety, etc.

  3. Difficult to deal with system problems when they occur so business operations are disrupted if the system is down for various reasons
    • Create manuals and response flows on what to do in the event of a system failure.
    • If a failure occurs on the server-side, the entire system may be shut down: To avoid it, it is essential to take measures such as duplicating servers and backing up data depending on the degree of impact.

  4. Security risks such as phishing, server attacks, and hacking
    • Security measures must be implemented on both the programmatic and infrastructure sides.
